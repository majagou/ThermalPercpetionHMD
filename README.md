# Thermal Perception in VR (HMD Version)

A research-driven VR project exploring how **visual design influences perceived temperature** in immersive environments.  
 
This repository presents two versions of the experience:
- [**CAVE Version**](https://github.com/majagou/ThermalPerception_CAVE) – designed for large-scale projection-based VR
- [**HMD Version**](https://github.com/majagou/ThermalPercpetionHMD) – designed for head-mounted display VR (Meta Quest 3)

The project investigates how environmental visuals such as **lighting, color, glare, steam, heat distortion, scene composition, and atmosphere** can shift users’ thermal perception, even when physical temperature remains unchanged.

## Visual Design Elements

To shape the feeling of warmth and coldness, the project uses several key visual design elements:

- **Landscape design** – different environment layouts and scene compositions for hot, cold, and neutral spaces  
- **Landscape materials** – terrain surfaces such as sand, snow, stone, and dry ground that visually suggest different temperatures  
- **Skymesh** – sky color, brightness, and weather mood used to reinforce thermal atmosphere  
- **Atmosphere** – fog, haze, steam, and other ambient effects that influence how the environment feels
